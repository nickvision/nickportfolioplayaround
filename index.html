From
Poems

What’s the point in arguing? The sunset was never as charming as we’d pretended; the water not glass-cut; the art boring. There is a layer of decoration that touches everything, a firmament we cannot break through. Waters above. Waters below. I said a few kind words before you went to take a bath. You returned stripped of the skin I’d once slept in. It wasn’t my fault you were allergic to feathers; the peppermint too stark; the bed cold. The architect must have been mad, to put shadows in such places; there’s dark quadrants in every part of the room that suck the dawn through a straw. For every infraction an appropriate reaction dredged from what once passed for care. I saw your coat hung up upon the stair. You knocked again at that door at the heart of the forest, regret powering your tiny fists. It was fur, definitely, the down like a freshly broken sparrow, worm still wriggling from its cold beak. So what if we couldn’t talk? The circus is a chorus. Genius hums the auditorium dead.

Hardening Truths

The Way of Things, 2015

Creativity held up some cue cards. The booze
held up many others. A pack of cigarettes
gets you in the club, but admittance doesn’t make you
happy, any more than the brief jolt of joy from a hedonistic twirl
across a dancefloor of regret.
Hear those echoes? It’s the clomping of the feet
of the past world in full retreat. Nobody is waving
a celebratory flag – we’re too smart for them,
too cynical by half. Yesterday
is a different country, and the directions
have come clean off the label at last. So just
wash hot. It’s all we can do.

Between Cycles

Recursive Lines, 2016

At the top of the stairs I was full of cares. I fell and, in falling, became myself again. It was not too wonderful, so it was believed by most. I’d been myself for years before, after all. And photos don’t lie she said. But my tongue wasn’t the trouble magnet – it was as likely a tourniquet as a noose. It made me feel very loose about the whole thing, the change heavy in my pocket, the sentence dragging across many years and mountains.
