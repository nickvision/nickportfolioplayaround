Bukowski

 

I guess the glory of Bukowski

was that he finally triumphed

unexpectedly

like a fire that burns

from wet logs

after all those years in the rain

swearing

angry

bitter and broken

the bottles and whores

that filled his life, the strife

of wives disappointed

the hours gurning away

in bleak post office halls

the dirty underwear

the yellowing paper

the typewriter epiphanies

that he would live past seventy

with a liver like that

with a face like that

with a mind like that

I think up in heaven

God

must be

laughing.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

An Anxious Dawn

 

I feel the dread forever place its hand upon my shoulder.

I feel the dread of me exude from every pore.

I abhor the ticking, abhor the stress that strains

Every taught and too tense limb. Clouds

Arise; I feel the air grow thin. Spring sunlight comes

And gelds what it should gild – my passions

Across another languid page are spilled. How I lie

Through nights turned deep, pulling, plumbing

The depths of self in search of sleep.

Hours curdle in the mind, thought's horses bolt

Screaming wild that "either/or" the soul compelled

Must in contemplation split itself apart –

The past yawns, the future gapes, yet the present

Will not fit itself in either one - the few dreams that remain

Are given over, surrendered; never to be done.

 

 

 

 

 

 

Sketches of an Infinite Cartographer

 

Something rose in the roof of your heart.

A sounding of uncertainty - what was it

That fired the soul of the person you

Used to be? Beauty

Is not an object, too long

A foreign concept.

Slow drink the morning's coffee and begin

Sketching out the staircase to

The heaven you still seek.

Here, the beams of wood may yet

Be beams of light, as sunlight filters through

The dancing leaves of the avenue's trees

And you remain, dreaming of new dreams.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Lost & Found

 

I am deadened saddened regularly affected by

the hard to shake disappointment of feeling

 

that despite it all, despite the friendships that

seem to be in their fundamental state both

 

a regularly expressed and yet still indefinable

connection, I fear nothing will ever be tangible

 

enough for us to hang ourselves on. The world

is full of kind and lovely people, but often

 

the sheer natural totality of their tepidity sets

my teeth on edge like a cold shower, suspecting

 

they will perhaps never get that which I am

forced to continually invest in the universal

 

nothingness, in order to reassure myself sufficiently

it is in fact otherwise. They and I trace fingers

 

in willows of thought shaken like cocktails

into complex and seemingly novel concoctions

 

in attempt to hide the bare fact that really we

are all only drinking to be drunk.         

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Breakfast

 

Darling, secrets should be kept close

to the chest, for the most part.

 

I sip the juice.

 

Breakfast, morning

like a cracked egg, so many

hours after dawn.

 

You crunch on your cereal.

 

Outside, cement mixers

turn, as birds

drop out the sky.

 

An omen, you say.

 

Just eat your breakfast, say I.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Half-cocked

 

The coin was lucky;

And lucky enough the beggar

Recognised it as so

And returned it to you, smiling.

Understanding, with no

Understanding –

The price of dreams is 5 złoty.

 

What am I and what are you?

The psychic circuitry of divine connection.

The collective sweat from both our brows.

The failure to find a fit within a fit.

The key that shudders and crumbles in the lock.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

On a Pathway Walking

 

All ostrich eyes and feet of clay you let reality

          slip trembling through your pale hands and clear-cut smiles

         and frowns that carved the sky in great circles of

Timorous starlight; I remember that dream under the Eiffel

          Tower, hair blowing in the wind, cold January moaning

          to the beat, and me, seeing you, through photographs

         smiling back and smiling back at you through a computer    

          screen

Hands poised with all those words I wished I’d typed all

          those things I wish had been said but will remain

          forever hanging

         in that dark place behind face and eyes where there is but    

          the taste of what happens next...

There is the patter of eager footsteps on lino and I have lost my            

          watch again for the fiftieth time after it was smashed

          that first time with you, fragments spinning across the

          beer-soaked floor

This eternal month of January has done for us both my love

          and we grow stronger in the shade of the months

          coming since – growing

         up and out and up              

          and out, eager for the light to take us in its arms again

And for it to be

          somehow different

          this time.
